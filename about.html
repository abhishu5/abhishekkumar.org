ABOUT

AI visibility is becoming an interpretation infrastructure problem.

I study how AI systems classify, retrieve, compress, and describe companies across conversational search environments. My work focuses on the structural reasons brands become misrepresented, averaged into generic categories, or excluded entirely from AI-generated answers.

WHY THIS EXISTS

Traditional SEO explains rankings. It does not explain AI interpretation.

The turning point came when companies with strong Google rankings started losing visibility inside AI systems. Their competitors were appearing inside ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, and AI Overviews. They were not.

The problem was not rankings. The problem was interpretation stability.

Most websites still describe themselves differently across pages, metadata, schema, LinkedIn, directories, and structured references. AI systems compress those fragmented signals into simplified category assumptions.

That compression layer is where visibility breaks.

RESEARCH FOCUS

Most companies do not have an AI visibility problem. They have a semantic consistency problem.

My work focuses on retrieval behaviour, entity stability, answer extractability, semantic chunking, cross-web reinforcement, and AI summarisation exposure.

The objective is not producing more content. The objective is ensuring AI systems arrive at stable interpretations across multiple retrieval environments.

01

Entity Stability

Whether AI systems consistently understand what category your company belongs to across homepage messaging, metadata, schema, citations, and retrieval layers.

02

Answer Extractability

Whether AI systems can accurately extract and summarise your company without hallucination, distortion, or semantic drift.

03

Cross-Web Consensus

Whether trusted external sources reinforce the same positioning across LinkedIn, Crunchbase, reviews, citations, and structured references.

04

Interpretation Consistency

Whether AI systems retrieve and describe your company similarly across conversational retrieval environments and recommendation queries.

POSITIONING
AI systems do not discover companies the way search engines ranked websites.
They retrieve fragments, compress meaning, reinforce consensus, and generate probabilistic interpretations. Visibility increasingly depends on whether your company becomes structurally understandable to machine reasoning systems.
