SERVICES

Most companies do not have an AI visibility problem. They have an interpretation consistency problem.

These diagnostics analyse how AI systems classify, retrieve, compress, and describe your company across conversational search environments. The objective is not rankings. The objective is stable machine interpretation across retrieval systems.

DIAGNOSTIC LAYERS

AI systems do not evaluate websites the way search engines historically did.

Modern retrieval systems synthesize fragmented information across webpages, directories, embeddings, structured data, citations, reviews, semantic relationships, and conversational memory layers. The audit framework evaluates where interpretation stability breaks.

01
Entity Stability Analysis
Measures whether AI systems consistently understand what category your company belongs to across homepage messaging, product pages, metadata, schema, and external references.
02
Answer Extractability
Evaluates whether AI systems can produce accurate answer-level summaries from your content without hallucination, averaging, or semantic drift.
03
Cross-Web Consensus
Analyses how external sources reinforce or destabilise your entity definition across LinkedIn, Crunchbase, press mentions, structured references, and category databases.
04
Retrieval Structure Analysis
Reviews whether your information architecture supports semantic chunking, passage-level retrieval, conversational extraction, and AI summarisation behaviour.
05
AI Response Divergence
Compares how ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, Perplexity, Copilot, and AI Overviews describe your company differently and identifies structural causes behind divergence.
06
Semantic Compression Risk
Identifies where ambiguous positioning causes AI systems to compress your company into broader generic categories that erase differentiation.
WHAT THE AUDIT IS

A structural diagnostic framework. Not SEO retainers. Not AI-content services.

Evidence-first analysis

Every conclusion is tied to observable retrieval behaviour, AI-generated outputs, semantic inconsistencies, or structural evidence gathered during the audit.

Interpretation-focused

The objective is not keyword positioning. The objective is whether AI systems accurately understand and describe the company.

Cross-system evaluation

The framework analyses how multiple AI systems behave simultaneously instead of relying on a single search engine perspective.

Retrieval-aware architecture

The audit evaluates how content structures behave inside retrieval pipelines, answer synthesis systems, semantic indexing layers, and conversational search flows.

PROCESS

How engagements work

STEP 01

Initial diagnostic intake

You submit your website, product positioning, primary category definition, and relevant public profiles. This establishes the initial entity map used for evaluation.

STEP 02

Multi-model retrieval analysis

AI systems are queried across multiple conversational environments to evaluate interpretation consistency, summarisation behaviour, recommendation appearance, and category alignment.

STEP 03

Structural diagnostic review

The audit analyses semantic structure, entity stability, retrieval reinforcement, answer extractability, cross-web consistency, and machine-readable infrastructure.

STEP 04

Diagnostic report delivery

You receive a structured diagnostic report containing evidence, interpretation risks, retrieval weaknesses, semantic inconsistencies, and prioritised structural corrections.

POSITIONING

AI visibility is becoming an interpretation infrastructure problem.

Most companies are still approaching AI visibility through traditional SEO assumptions: more content, more keywords, more optimisation activity. But retrieval systems increasingly depend on semantic coherence, extractability, reinforcement consistency, and entity stability across distributed information environments.

The companies that become consistently visible across AI systems will not necessarily be the companies publishing the most content. They will be the companies easiest for AI systems to interpret correctly.
